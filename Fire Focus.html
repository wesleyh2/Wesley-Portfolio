Fire Focus Desk Pet

Designing a desk pet to provide a physical interruption to doomscrolling and task paralysis

Role

Design Lead

Timeline

October 2024

Team
Noah Purow - Engineer/Designer
Skills
Sketching Arduino Coding Animation

Overview

This project explores how playful, physical interactions can disrupt “scrolling paralysis,” the state of endless, passive consumption on social media. We designed a fireplace-inspired desk pet: a Tamagotchi-style companion that lives on your desk as a miniature hearth with a lively animated flame. The fire thrives when the user stays balanced, but as time spent on apps like Instagram, TikTok, or YouTube increases, the flame weakens and shrinks, sending gentle reminders. To revive it, the user must complete a short rhythm-based interaction that mimics fanning the flame.

Background

Scrolling paralysis is an increasingly common phenomenon, particularly among younger people and those with ADHD. Existing screen time tools often fail because they are digital-only and easy to dismiss. Our goal was to create a playful yet effective physical interruption that encourages balance while giving users a sense of care and companionship.

Current Solutions

Many existing screen time management solutions are app-based, such as Apple's Screen Time, which forces interruptions to break scrolling cycles. While effective in theory, these lack an emotional hook and are often ignored. By drawing on the nostalgia of Tamagotchis and the warmth of a digital pet, our approach creates emotional investment and a physical interruption, users don’t just dismiss a timer, they care for a real companion.

The Idea

We imagined a desk pet shaped like a miniature fireplace with an anthropomorphic flame inspired by Calcifer from Howl’s Moving Castle. As the user scrolls, the flame dims and shrinks, signaling distress. To revive it, the user must complete a playful rhythm-based game that mirrors tossing on new firewood—transforming screen time management into an act of care.

Sketch Exploration

Early sketches explored different hearth shapes, character designs, and possible interaction methods. These iterations helped refine how the pet should look and feel on a desk—approachable, and charming. I also explored the general user flow through sketching.

Final Concept & Renders

The final concept is a desk-sized fireplace with a digital display showing the animated flame. The character visibly grows, flickers, and dims in response to the user’s app activity. We created 3D renders and sample procedural flame animations to bring the design to life.
